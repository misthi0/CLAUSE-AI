URGENT CONTRACT - CRITICAL ISSUES IDENTIFIED

This agreement contains several problematic clauses that pose significant risks:

PAYMENT TERMS: Payment required upfront with no refund clause. No escrow protection. Vendor has complete control over funds before any work begins.

TERMINATION: No termination rights for the client. Vendor can terminate at any time without notice or cause. Client must continue paying regardless of vendor performance or delivery.

LIABILITY: Client assumes all liability for any damages, losses, or claims. Vendor has zero liability even for gross negligence or willful misconduct. Indemnification clause heavily favors vendor.

INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY: All work product, including client's pre-existing IP, transfers to vendor immediately. Client has no ownership rights to anything created during the engagement.

DISPUTE RESOLUTION: Mandatory arbitration in vendor's home jurisdiction. Client waives right to class action or jury trial. All arbitration costs paid by client regardless of outcome.

CONFIDENTIALITY: Vendor can disclose client's confidential information at their discretion. No NDA protections. Trade secrets not protected.

FORCE MAJEURE: Extremely broad force majeure clause that excuses vendor from nearly all obligations. Client still required to pay during force majeure events.

FINANCIAL TERMS: Total contract value: $500,000 with 100% upfront payment. No milestone-based payments. No performance guarantees. No service level agreements.

REGULATORY COMPLIANCE: Contract lacks required data privacy clauses. No GDPR compliance. No cybersecurity requirements. Potential regulatory violations.

This contract heavily favors the vendor and poses extreme financial, legal, and operational risks to the client.
